# Beginner Piano Songs

Curated for an advanced-beginner level — you can read both clefs, handle simple chords + melody, but advanced jazz voicings are still a stretch.

## What's in here

14 pieces across 6 buckets. Each piece has its own folder with a `.txt` file containing:
- Composer + year
- Difficulty notes
- Legal sources to buy/download
- Key, tempo, structure
- Practice tips

## Suggested learning order

If you want a path of least resistance:

1. **Gymnopédie No. 1** (Satie) — slow, sparse, gorgeous, gentle introduction
2. **Cantaloupe Island** (Hancock) — 4-note riff, instant gratification
3. **Christmas Time Is Here** — beautiful chord changes, slow tempo
4. **Peace Piece** (Evans) — two-chord LH ostinato, freedom in RH
5. **Gnossienne No. 1** (Satie) — slightly more notes, same vibe
6. **Song for My Father** (Silver) — locks in a Latin groove
7. **Isn't She Lovely** — pop chord progression, sing along
8. ...then onto the rest

## Trusted sources for the copyrighted stuff

- **musicnotes.com** — Single-song PDFs, usually $5-7. Good search.
- **sheetmusicplus.com** — Songbooks + singles. Real Books for jazz.
- **sheetmusicdirect.com** — Hal Leonard's official store.
- **Hal Leonard Real Book** (vol. 1-3) — covers most jazz standards as lead sheets. Buy once, use forever.
- **Stevie Wonder Anthology** (Hal Leonard) — covers Isn't She Lovely + many others.
- Your **local library** often has jazz fake books and Stevie Wonder songbooks you can scan or borrow.
