Gnossienne No. 1
================

Composer: Erik Satie
Year: 1890
Status: PUBLIC DOMAIN — free legal download

Key: F minor (modal, no key signature in original — Satie used no time signature OR bar lines)
Time: Free / 4/4 in modern editions
Tempo: Lent — slow and meditative
Difficulty: Advanced beginner. Slightly trickier than the Gymnopédies but still very accessible.

Why include it
--------------
- Famous, atmospheric, "exotic" sound — uses the Phrygian mode
- LH is a steady gentle ostinato (low bass + chord, same as Gymnopédies)
- RH melody has a few more notes and ornaments than the Gymnopédies
- Great for developing rubato — Satie wrote weird French instructions like
  "Très perdu" (very lost) and "Postulez en vous-même" (question yourself)
  in place of normal dynamics

Where to download (free, legal)
-------------------------------
- IMSLP: https://imslp.org/wiki/Trois_Gnossiennes_(Satie%2C_Erik)
  (download the "Complete Score" — has all 3 Gnossiennes)
- Mutopia Project: https://www.mutopiaproject.org/cgibin/make-table.cgi?Composer=SatieE
- 8notes (free PDF + MIDI): https://www.8notes.com/scores/22922.asp

Practice tips
-------------
1. The original has NO bar lines — modern editions add them. Get an edition
   with bar lines while you're learning, then try the original later for fun.
2. The LH ostinato never stops — practice it until it's automatic, then add RH
3. Take liberties with the timing. This piece breathes more than the Gymnopédies.
4. Reference recording: Aldo Ciccolini or Anne Queffélec
