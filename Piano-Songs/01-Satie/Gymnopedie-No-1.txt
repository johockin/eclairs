Gymnopédie No. 1
================

Composer: Erik Satie
Year: 1888
Status: PUBLIC DOMAIN — free legal download

Key: D major (with a wandering, almost modal feel)
Time: 3/4
Tempo: Lent et douloureux (slow and painful) — quarter note ≈ 60 bpm
Difficulty: Advanced beginner. Famously approachable. Slow tempo, sparse texture.

Why it's a great starting piece
-------------------------------
- LH plays just two alternating chords per measure (a low bass note then a chord)
- RH melody moves slowly, mostly stepwise
- No fast passages, no hand crossings, no complex rhythms
- The hardest thing is *playing it slowly enough* and shaping the melody musically

Where to download (free, legal)
-------------------------------
- IMSLP: https://imslp.org/wiki/Trois_Gymnop%C3%A9dies_(Satie%2C_Erik)
  (download the "Complete Score" — has all 3 Gymnopédies)
- Mutopia Project: https://www.mutopiaproject.org/cgibin/make-table.cgi?Composer=SatieE
- 8notes (free PDF + MIDI): https://www.8notes.com/scores/630.asp

Practice tips
-------------
1. Learn LH alone first. The pattern is: low bass note (beat 1) → chord (beats 2 and 3).
   The bass walks down the scale slowly across the piece.
2. Use the sustain pedal lightly — change with each LH chord.
3. Don't rush the melody. Each phrase wants to breathe.
4. Pianist Pascal Rogé's recording is a great reference for tempo and touch.
