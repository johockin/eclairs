Waltz for Debby
===============

Composer: Bill Evans (music) / Gene Lees (lyrics)
Year: 1956
Status: COPYRIGHTED — placeholder. Purchase or borrow legally.

Key: F major (modulates to C, then back)
Time: 3/4
Tempo: Medium waltz, around quarter note ≈ 130 bpm (Evans played it slower)
Difficulty: The full Bill Evans solo piano version is INTERMEDIATE-PLUS.
            For an advanced beginner, look for:
            (a) Lead sheet (Real Book) — melody + chord symbols only
            (b) "Easy piano" arrangements — simplified RH/LH

Why include it
--------------
- One of Evans' most beautiful and beloved compositions
- A waltz, written for his niece — has an innocent, lyrical quality
- The melody alone is gorgeous and worth learning
- Once you can play the melody, you can comp simple LH chords from the lead
  sheet — that's a real "playing jazz" experience

Where to buy/download (legal)
-----------------------------
- **The Real Book Vol. 1 (6th edition, Hal Leonard)** — has the lead sheet
  This is THE book for jazz tunes. Buy it once, use it for life. ~$40.
- Musicnotes.com: search "Waltz for Debby" — there are easy piano arrangements
- Hal Leonard "Bill Evans Plays Standards" — transcription of Evans' solo version
  (save for when you're more advanced)
- Library: most public libraries have at least one Real Book

Practice tips
-------------
1. From the lead sheet: play the melody RH only first. It's a big interval jump
   in the first phrase — practice it slowly.
2. Then add LH "guide tones" — just the root of each chord, on beat 1 of each
   measure
3. Then add the 5th of each chord on beat 2 or 3
4. That's enough to sound like jazz — you don't need shell voicings yet
5. Reference recording: Bill Evans Trio "Waltz for Debby" (1961, Riverside) —
   the live Village Vanguard recording. Iconic.
