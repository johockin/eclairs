Peace Piece
===========

Composer: Bill Evans
Year: 1958 (from the album "Everybody Digs Bill Evans")
Status: COPYRIGHTED — placeholder. Purchase or borrow legally.

Key: C major (modal — feels like C Lydian)
Time: 4/4
Tempo: Very slow, around quarter note ≈ 60 bpm
Difficulty: This is the EASIEST genuine Bill Evans piece. Truly approachable for
            an advanced beginner.

Why it works for a beginner
---------------------------
- LH is a TWO-CHORD OSTINATO that repeats the entire piece
  (Cmaj7 → G7 voicings, but you can simplify to just C → G)
- RH is improvised — Evans plays the same gentle melodic ideas over and over,
  slowly building density. You can play your own version.
- Slow tempo. No bar pressure. It's basically meditation.
- This piece literally inspired Miles Davis' "Flamenco Sketches" — modal jazz
  with no chord changes is freedom for a beginner

Where to buy/download (legal)
-----------------------------
- Musicnotes.com: search "Peace Piece Bill Evans"
- Hal Leonard "Bill Evans Plays Standards" or
  "The Bill Evans Collection" — transcription books
- "Bill Evans: 19 Solo Pieces" (Hal Leonard, ISBN 9781476868981)
- Note: there isn't an "easy piano" arrangement of this — but the original
  is genuinely playable because it's so sparse and slow

Practice tips
-------------
1. Master the LH ostinato first — it should sound like a heartbeat
2. RH: start with just a single note melody, no chords. Use only white keys at
   first. Listen to the LH and respond.
3. Don't try to copy Evans note-for-note. The point of this piece is your own
   improvisation over the ostinato.
4. Reference recording: the original 1958 take. It's 6:30 of pure space.
   Listen first without trying to play.
