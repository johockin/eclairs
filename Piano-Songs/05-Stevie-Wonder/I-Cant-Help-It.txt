I Can't Help It
===============

Composers: Stevie Wonder & Susaye Greene
Performed by: Michael Jackson (on "Off the Wall," 1979)
Status: COPYRIGHTED — placeholder. Purchase or borrow legally.

Key: F major (the recording is in F, but uses gorgeous jazz changes)
Time: 4/4
Tempo: Medium, around quarter note ≈ 117 bpm (laid-back)
Difficulty: Trickier than the others on this list. The chord changes are
            JAZZY — Fmaj7, Bbmaj7, Em7, A7, Dm7, Gm7 territory. Get an easy
            piano arrangement to start.

Why it's worth the climb
------------------------
- One of the most beautiful songs ever written for "Off the Wall"
- Produced by Quincy Jones — those chord voicings are jazz-school perfect
- A direct line from Stevie's harmonic vocabulary into Michael's pop world
- Once you can play this, you can play almost any neo-soul song

Practice tips
-------------
1. The chord changes are the hardest part — make a chord chart of just the
   symbols (Fmaj7 / Bbmaj7 / Em7 / A7 / Dm7) and practice transitioning between
   them in LH first, with RH playing the melody
2. Don't worry about jazz voicings yet. Use simple LH triads + 7ths
3. The song's groove is half-time — feel the snare on beat 3 only
4. Reference recording: Michael Jackson, "Off the Wall" (1979) — original
   Quincy Jones production. Stevie's demo also exists if you can find it.
5. Optional: study Robert Glasper's solo piano cover for inspiration on
   what's possible once you advance
