Isn't She Lovely
================

Composer: Stevie Wonder
Year: 1976 (from "Songs in the Key of Life")
Status: COPYRIGHTED — placeholder. Purchase or borrow legally.

Key: E major (original recording — but easy versions often transpose to C or G)
Time: 4/4
Tempo: Medium-up, around quarter note ≈ 120 bpm
Difficulty: Advanced beginner. The chord changes use II-V-I patterns but
            don't require complex voicings. Lots of "easy piano" versions exist.

Why it's an easy win
--------------------
- Cyclical chord progression — the verse just loops 4 chords (Em7-A7-Dmaj7-Gmaj7
  → in E major this becomes II-V-I-IV)
- The melody is singable and the rhythm isn't too tricky
- Everyone knows it. You play this at any family gathering and people smile.
- Perfect first "real Stevie" song

Practice tips
-------------
1. The easy piano version in C or G is friendlier than the original E major
2. The chord pattern repeats — once you have the verse, you have most of the song
3. The little chromatic descending bridge ("She's so I really love her...")
   is the hardest part. Work on it separately.
4. The original recording has a long harmonica solo and baby noises (Stevie's
   daughter Aisha) — don't worry about replicating those. Just play the song.
5. Reference recording: Stevie Wonder, "Songs in the Key of Life" (1976, Tamla)
