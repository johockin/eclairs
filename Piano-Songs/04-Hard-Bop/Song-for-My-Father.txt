Song for My Father
==================

Composer: Horace Silver
Year: 1964
Status: COPYRIGHTED — placeholder. Purchase or borrow legally.

Key: F minor
Time: 4/4 (Latin / bossa-nova feel)
Tempo: Medium, around quarter note ≈ 130 bpm
Difficulty: Advanced beginner with the lead sheet. The HEAD (main melody) is
            very accessible. Improvising over it is harder.

Why it's a perfect first hard-bop tune
--------------------------------------
- The LH bass line is one of the most iconic riffs in jazz — and it's just
  4 notes repeated! (F-C-Eb-Db, give or take)
- Steely Dan ripped it off for "Rikki Don't Lose That Number" — you already
  know the groove
- The head (melody) is bluesy, simple, and memorable
- Latin/bossa-nova rhythm is steady — no swing weirdness to worry about

Where to buy/download (legal)
-----------------------------
- **The Real Book Vol. 1 (Hal Leonard)** — has the lead sheet. Essential.
- Musicnotes.com: search "Song for My Father Silver"
  - Piano Solo and Easy Piano versions exist
- Hal Leonard "Horace Silver Collection" — transcriptions of his classics
- Sher Music "The New Real Book" series

Practice tips
-------------
1. Learn the LH bass riff first. It's 2 bars long and repeats. Just lock it in
   like a metronome.
2. The RH head is a bluesy minor pentatonic melody. Mostly F minor pentatonic
   (F-Ab-Bb-C-Eb) plus occasional B natural for tension.
3. Start with just the head played twice through with the bass. That's the
   whole structure of the tune — head, then solos, then head out.
4. Reference recording: Horace Silver, "Song for My Father" album (1965, Blue Note)
