Mercy, Mercy, Mercy
===================

Composer: Joe Zawinul
Recorded by: Cannonball Adderley Quintet
Year: 1966
Status: COPYRIGHTED — placeholder. Purchase or borrow legally.

Key: F major
Time: 4/4 (gospel / soul jazz feel)
Tempo: Slow-medium, around quarter note ≈ 76 bpm
Difficulty: Advanced beginner. The slowest of the hard-bop heads in this
            collection — very approachable.

Why it's lovable
----------------
- Gospel feel — slow, churchy chord changes
- The melody is singable. You can hum it after one listen.
- A #1 hit single (#11 on the pop charts!) — proof that jazz can be popular
- Joe Zawinul (later of Weather Report) wrote it on a Wurlitzer electric piano
- Beautiful changes in F: F → Bb → C → F → Dm → Bb → C → F (basic gospel)

Practice tips
-------------
1. Slow tempo = good for an advanced beginner. Use it as your "groove practice"
   piece — focus on locking with a metronome
2. The melody is basically F major pentatonic with bluesy bends. RH plays
   simple, LH plays gospel chord changes (just root + 5th in LH is enough)
3. Triads are fine — you don't need 7th chords to make this sound right
4. Reference recording: Cannonball Adderley Quintet, "Mercy, Mercy, Mercy!"
   (1966, Capitol) — recorded LIVE in front of a crowd, you can hear them
   clapping along
