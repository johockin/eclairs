Cantaloupe Island
=================

Composer: Herbie Hancock
Year: 1964 (from the album "Empyrean Isles")
Status: COPYRIGHTED — placeholder. Purchase or borrow legally.

Key: F minor
Time: 4/4
Tempo: Medium, around quarter note ≈ 100 bpm
Difficulty: Probably the most beginner-friendly jazz head ever written.
            Genuinely playable by an advanced beginner.

Why it's perfect
----------------
- The main RIFF is just 4 chords stabbed in a syncopated pattern
- The structure is dead simple: 16 bars, 3 chords (Fm7 / Db7 / Dm7 / Fm7)
- US3 sampled it for "Cantaloop" in 1993 — you already know it from hip-hop
- Once you have the riff, you have a "real jazz tune" you can play

Where to buy/download (legal)
-----------------------------
- **The Real Book Vol. 1 (Hal Leonard)** — has the lead sheet
- Musicnotes.com: search "Cantaloupe Island Hancock"
- Hal Leonard "Herbie Hancock Classic Jazz Compositions" songbook
- Sher Music "The New Real Book"

Practice tips
-------------
1. Learn the chord stabs in RH first. They're rhythmic — the rhythm IS the song.
   Practice with a metronome.
2. LH plays the bass note of each chord on beat 1
3. Once you have the head, try improvising over the changes using F minor
   pentatonic (F-Ab-Bb-C-Eb) — works on all 3 chords
4. Reference recording: Herbie Hancock, "Empyrean Isles" (1964, Blue Note)
   — also the US3 version "Cantaloop (Flip Fantasia)" if you want the funk angle
