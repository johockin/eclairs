The Sidewinder
==============

Composer: Lee Morgan
Year: 1963
Status: COPYRIGHTED — placeholder. Purchase or borrow legally.

Key: F major (a 24-bar blues, not 12)
Time: 4/4 (boogaloo / Latin shuffle feel)
Tempo: Medium, around quarter note ≈ 132 bpm
Difficulty: Advanced beginner with the lead sheet. The head is bluesy and
            rhythmic — accessible.

Why include it
--------------
- Defined the "soul jazz / boogaloo" sound — irresistibly groovy
- Lee Morgan was a hard-bop trumpeter and this is his most famous tune
- The head is built on the F blues scale plus a few hip chromatic passing notes
- Bass line is a great LH workout

Where to buy/download (legal)
-----------------------------
- **The Real Book Vol. 1 (Hal Leonard)** — has the lead sheet
- Musicnotes.com: search "The Sidewinder Morgan"
- Hal Leonard "The Lee Morgan Collection" — transcriptions
- Sher Music "The New Real Book"

Practice tips
-------------
1. The bass line walks — practice it LH alone, in time, until smooth
2. The head has some chromatic notes that may sting your fingers — go slow
3. The rhythmic feel is shuffled / boogaloo — listen to the recording before
   trying to read it. Your ears will teach you the feel faster than your eyes.
4. Improvise with F major blues scale (F-G-Ab-A-Bb-C-Eb-F) over the form
5. Reference recording: Lee Morgan, "The Sidewinder" (1964, Blue Note)
