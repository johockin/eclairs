Christmas Time Is Here
======================

Composer: Vince Guaraldi (music) / Lee Mendelson (lyrics)
Year: 1965 (from "A Charlie Brown Christmas")
Status: COPYRIGHTED — placeholder. Purchase or borrow legally.

Key: F major (original recording is in F)
Time: 3/4
Tempo: Slow waltz, around quarter note ≈ 60-70 bpm
Difficulty: Advanced beginner. The melody is simple; the chord changes are jazzy
            but a simplified arrangement makes it very playable.

Why it's beautiful
------------------
- The chord changes are achingly pretty (Fmaj7 → Bb7 → Em7b5 → A7 territory)
- Slow tempo gives you time to think
- One of the most loved Christmas songs of the past 60 years
- Great introduction to "jazz piano feels" without scary technical demands

Where to buy/download (legal)
-----------------------------
- Musicnotes.com: search "Christmas Time Is Here Guaraldi"
  - Easy Piano arrangement available (~$5)
  - Original/Pro arrangement also available (~$6)
- SheetMusicPlus.com: same searches, often comes in the
  "A Charlie Brown Christmas" songbook
- Hal Leonard "A Charlie Brown Christmas" songbook (~$17, has all the famous tunes)
- Local library: many libraries have this songbook

Practice tips
-------------
1. Start with the EASY PIANO arrangement, not the transcription of the
   original recording (Guaraldi's voicings are gorgeous but advanced).
2. Learn the melody with simple block chords in LH first
3. The bridge ("Oh that we could always see / Such spirit through the year")
   is harder than the verse — practice it separately
4. Reference recording: the original Vince Guaraldi Trio version, of course
